Change in populations over time
Study online at https://quizlet.com/_d6dx6n
1. environment: the surroundings or conditions in which an organism lives.
2. natural selection: A process in which individuals that have certain inherited traits tend to survive and reproduce at higher rates than other individuals because of those traits.
3. Fitness: Ability of an organism to survive and reproduce in its environment
4. Variation: Any difference between individuals of the same species.
5. competition: the struggle between organisms to survive in a habitat with limited resources
6. Adaptation: A trait that helps an organism survive and reproduce
7. Heredity: Passing of traits from parents to offspring
8. Mutation: a random error in gene replication that leads to a change. It may be harmful, neutral or beneficial.
9. Prokaryote: A unicellular organism that lacks a nucleus and membrane bound organelles
10. Eukaryote: A cell that contains a nucleus and membrane bound organelles
11. Reproduction: the process of producing offspring
12. Trait: A characteristic that an organism can pass on to its offspring through its genes.
13. Gene: A segment of DNA on a chromosome that codes for a specific trait
14. Population: A group of individuals that belong to the same species and live in the same area
15. Organism: A living thing
16. Species: A group of similar organisms that can breed and produce fertile offspring.
17. Mitosis: cell division in which the nucleus divides into nuclei containing the same number of chromosomes
18. binary fission: A form of asexual reproduction in single-celled organisms by which one cell divides into two cells of the same size
